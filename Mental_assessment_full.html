⚠️ IMPORTANT: This tool is for educational and informational purposes only. It utilizes questions from peer-reviewed research scales (LSRP, PQ-16, MSI-BPD) but cannot diagnose any condition. High scores indicate a match with certain trait patterns, not a clinical pathology.

Psychopathy Traits

Based on the Levenson Self-Report Psychopathy Scale (LSRP). This measures primary psychopathy (callousness, manipulation) and secondary psychopathy (impulsivity).

1. Success is based on survival of the fittest; I am not concerned about the losers.
2. I find myself in the same kinds of trouble, time after time.
3. I feel no guilt when I lie to someone.
4. My main purpose in life is getting as many goodies as I can.

Psychosis Screen (Prodromal)

Adapted from the Prodromal Questionnaire (PQ-16). These questions screen for "positive symptoms" like perceptual abnormalities and unusual thought content.

1. Have you seen things that other people cannot see?
2. Do you sometimes feel that people are talking about you or watching you?
3. Have you heard unusual sounds like banging, clicking, or voices that others couldn't hear?
4. Do you feel that your thoughts are being read by others or broadcast out loud?

Emotional Instability

Adapted from the McLean Screening Instrument for BPD (MSI-BPD). This assesses traits related to affective instability and relationship volatility.

1. Have your relationships been like a rollercoaster? (Lots of ups and downs)
2. Do you often feel empty inside?
3. Have you made desperate efforts to avoid being abandoned by someone you love?
4. Do your moods change very quickly (e.g., depression or anger lasting a few hours)?
