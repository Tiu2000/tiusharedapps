1. Total Contributed (The Sweat Equity)
This is the actual cash you took out of your paycheck. This represents the nights you stayed in, the coffees you made at home, and the discipline you kept. This is the money you worked for.

2. Step-by-Step: Where do I put the money?

You can't just stuff cash under your mattress. Inflation will eat it. Here is the "Order of Operations" for your paycheck.

1

Build the "Oh No" Fund

What is it? An Emergency Fund.

Why? Cars break. Laptops die. Jobs get lost. Before you invest, save 3-6 months of basic expenses in a regular High-Yield Savings Account.

Goal: $5,000 - $10,000 (Keep this safe and liquid).

2

The Employer Match (Free Money)

What is it? A 401(k) match.

How it works: If your job offers a 401(k), they might say "We match 3%." This means if you put in 3% of your salary, they give you another 3% for free.

Analogy: Imagine buying a $1 soda and getting a second one instantly for free. That's a 100% return immediately.

3

The Roth IRA (The Tax Shield)

What is it? An Individual Retirement Account.

Why? You pay taxes on the money now while you are young (and in a lower tax bracket). When you pull the money out at retirement, it is 100% Tax-Free.

Action: Open a Roth IRA at a broker (like Fidelity, Vanguard, or Schwab). Buy a simple "Index Fund" (like the S&P 500).

4

General Investing (The Bridge)

What is it? A standard brokerage account.

Why? 401(k)s and IRAs lock your money up until you are roughly 60 years old. If you want to buy a house in 10 years, put the extra money here.
